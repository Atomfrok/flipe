What is Scalping?


Flipe -In trading, the stock scalping technique that traders use is buying lots of stocks and selling them as soon as price gaps become visible


Scalping is a strategy chosen by many traders who want to make quick profits. Even though it is similar to day trading, scalping tends to have a larger capital. In addition, day trading is usually done by traders every day. In contrast to scalping (scalping practitioners) who only trade in a very short time and are not done every day.



Scalpers take advantage of small movements of a certain object to make money. Scalping trading can be a very profitable method if a trader is able to apply the right exit strategy.


However, there are also downsides to using scalping as a trading strategy. Scalping involves a large number of transactions compared to other strategies. Opening a large number of trading transactions will incur higher transaction costs because you will have to pay a commission for each trade.


Scalping Strengths and Weaknesses


As an option, scalping is a strategy that has advantages and disadvantages. The weakness of scalping is that the technique is difficult and requires precision. In addition, the trader must be constantly in front of the screen while trading.


The advantage of scalping is its ability to generate profits in a short time. Unlike the long term, which has to wait for days, with scalping you can get trading profits on the same day.


Another advantage of scalping is the ease of correcting mistakes. when you realize the wrong open position, you can identify it. Then you can choose to close the old open position and open a new position.



Here are tips to help you carry out the scalping technique well.


1. Start Slowly from Swing Trading First


Before practicing scalping directly on the exchange, try to get comfortable with swing trading first. This strategy is at least in the middle of the trading level because the transaction time range is medium, which is in a matter of days to weeks.


Swing trading is looser than scalping because you don't have to keep watching the stock market in real time. If you are used to swings, you can shift slowly to scalping by narrowing the timeframe between buying and selling stocks.


2. Sharpen Ability with Small Capital


Small capital is very important in the early days of using stock scalping techniques. Even if the returns are small, Smart People will protect you from possible big losses while testing your technical analysis skills.


These stock trading tips are also useful so that Smart People don't target large price differences and stay away from actual scalping practices.


3. Take advantage of Screening Outside Trading Time


Through stock screening, Smart People can filter stocks that match your criteria. The use of special software will certainly help save a lot of time and avoid subjective factors in choosing.


Veteran scalper Bekti Sutikna suggests screening at night. At this time, Smart People can prepare stock data that is ready to be entered once the stock exchange re-opens every day.

5. Avoid Margin Transactions and Short Selling


Always ready if at any time Smart People loses and does not return on investment. That is why traders need to stay away from what is called margin or borrow funds from brokers. The same is true for short selling, which is in practice selling credit stock from brokers and services back at a low price.


Maximizing returns is okay. However, mastering stock scalping techniques is more important when you start doing it even though the results are very small.
